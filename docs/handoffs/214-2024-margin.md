# HO 214 — Rich card, part 3: 2024 House margin (scoped scrape, House-only)

## Why

Gives the HO 210 expanded race-card row its result bar — the last "buildable after backfill" field from the HO 211 diagnostic, greenlit by the HO 213 Part B probe. Challenger name (HO 213) + 2024 margin (this) is the expanded card finally fully populated for the House seats. Slots into the result-bar insertion point already stubbed in `RaceMapCard.tsx`.

The HO 213 probe did the hard verification — this is a scoped build against a confirmed source, with two failure modes locked out below.

## The confirmed spec (from the HO 213 Part B probe — do NOT re-derive)

- **Source:** per-seat 2024 election pages — `…/{State}'s_{N}th_Congressional_District_election,_2024` — NOT the cached 2026 pages (those carry only 2026 voteboxes + a 2024-presidential-by-district section, which is a *different metric* and must never be read for this).
- **Isolation:** the **"General election for U.S. House"** votebox header isolates the 2024 general from everything else on the page. HO 206's `percentage_number` / bar-width extractor reads the candidate shares. Margin = winner% − runner-up%.

## Three constraints — bake these into the build, they are not optional

1. **House-only (≤108 races).** The 29 Senate index seats last ran in 2020 (FL/OH special seats 2022), not 2024 — a "2024 margin" does not exist for them. Scope the scrape to House seats in `getRacesIndex` only. **Senate rows render NO margin bar, by design** — same null-safe absence as HO 212 cash on uncovered seats. A Senate row with no bar is correct, not a gap.

2. **Live fetch + browser UA.** curl/WebFetch get a ~51KB bot-challenge stub; node `fetch` with a browser User-Agent works. 2024 pages are NOT cached → ~108 live fetches. Reuse the primaries-scraper fetch idiom + a browser UA. Be polite (the same pacing the primaries scrape uses); this is a one-time backfill, not a hot path.

3. **Per-seat URL resolution (this is where gaps hide).** Page naming varies — TX-15's obvious title 404'd, ME-02 resolved. The build needs a URL-resolution step, NOT a string template. **And it must REPORT which seats resolved vs. 404'd, not silently drop them** — a seat whose URL doesn't resolve gets no margin, and we need to see that list so the coverage gap is visible, not invisible. Same honest-gap discipline as everywhere in this project.

## The two failure modes — locked out

**Wrong-votebox (the HO 206 gotcha, it WILL bite here if ignored).** These 2024 pages carry many voteboxes — the probe found 418 on ME-02, including 2024 *primaries* alongside the general. A page-wide `percentage_number` grab pulls the wrong election (this is exactly what bit primaries results ingestion in HO 206). **Isolate the "General election for U.S. House" votebox specifically; do NOT scan the page.** Reuse HO 206's header-scoped extraction approach, not a broad selector. Verify on a known seat that the margin written matches the 2024 *general* result, not a primary.

**Silent URL drops.** A 404'd seat must appear in the report as unresolved, never just be absent from the results with no trace. The backfill prints resolved-count / 404-count / the actual 404 list.

## Build

**Schema:** add a `margin_2024` column (REAL, the winner−runner-up percentage-point margin; signed or unsigned — unsigned magnitude is fine for a bar, but note which) to the appropriate table — likely `races` (it's a per-seat 2024 fact, 1:1 with the race). Confirm the right table by reading the live schema; nullable, NULL = no 2024 general (Senate) or unresolved URL.

**Backfill script** (`scripts/backfill-2024-margin.ts` or similar, matching the existing backfill-script pattern):
- For each House seat in `getRacesIndex(2026)`: resolve the 2024 page URL, fetch live (browser UA), isolate the "General election for U.S. House" votebox, extract winner + runner-up shares, compute margin, write `margin_2024`.
- Senate seats: skip (no write, stays NULL).
- Report: House seats processed, resolved vs 404 (with the 404 list), margins written, and a spot-check of 2–3 known results (e.g. ME-02 Golden vs Theriault) to prove the *general* was read, not a primary.
- Idempotent / re-runnable (overwrite on re-run, like the other backfills).

**Card render (`RaceMapCard.tsx`):** the expanded-row result-bar insertion point renders a 2024 margin bar where `margin_2024` is non-null — a simple two-segment or single-magnitude bar (winner margin), labeled (e.g. `2024: R+8.2` or `2024 margin 8.2pts` — match the card's existing label style and the design intent). NULL → no bar (Senate, or unresolved House seat), clean omit, no `NaN`/empty.

## Gates (HO 210 invariants — must not regress)

- Coloring functions (`racesFill`/`primariesFill`) untouched — payload + a new column, not a map change.
- count==list holds — margin touches neither the tile count nor `getRacesIndex`.

## Verification

- `tsc` clean; `/races` 200; **stylesheet loads** (verify the asset per the HO 212 lesson — rebuild if the dev server's been up a while; don't trust a bare 200).
- **Wrong-votebox check (the gate):** a known House seat (ME-02) shows the 2024 *general* margin (Golden vs Theriault), NOT a 2024 primary result. Print the extracted shares to prove it.
- Coverage report: resolved vs 404 House seats, with the 404 list visible.
- A House race with a resolved margin shows the bar in its expanded row; a Senate race shows NO bar (by design); a 404'd House seat shows no bar (and is on the reported list).
- Null vs real: confirm no `NaN`/empty bar anywhere.
- Gates: coloring fns separate; count==list unchanged.
- Eyeball: expand a House race (margin bar + HO 213 challenger + HO 212 cash all coexist in one expanded row — the fully-populated card) and a Senate race (cash present, no margin bar, no challenger-or-placeholder per its data).
